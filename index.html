About the Lab

Research for more accessible and data-informed urban futures

The Urban Geo-Innovation Lab explores how geospatial data science and artificial intelligence can improve the way cities are planned, measured, and understood. We work at the intersection of GIScience, urban mobility, pedestrian environments, and spatial equity, with a particular interest in vulnerable populations and real-world decision support.

Research Themes

Core directions of the lab

GeoAI & Spatial Machine Learning

Developing intelligent geospatial methods to analyze complex urban systems using machine learning, deep learning, and network-based models.

Urban Mobility & Active Transportation

Studying walking, cycling, and micro-mobility patterns to support healthier, safer, and more sustainable cities.

Accessibility & Spatial Equity

Evaluating how urban environments serve vulnerable populations, including people with disabilities, older adults, and underserved communities.

Urban Sensing & Geospatial Data Fusion

Combining OpenStreetMap, remote sensing, LiDAR, field surveys, and mobility data to build richer and more actionable urban intelligence.

Projects

Selected work

  • Pedestrian accessibility modelling for vulnerable populations
  • GeoAI methods for walkability and street-level urban analysis
  • Human navigation and route-choice research in urban environments
  • Multimodal geospatial data integration for sustainable transportation

Why it matters

Our research aims to move from descriptive mapping toward decision-support systems that can help governments, planners, and researchers design urban environments that are safer, fairer, and more supportive of active transportation.
